pulsarcfirst

Sat Jan 23, 2016 in using tags

I’ve realized one of the reasons I develop games is to emulate that same sense of excitement and adventure that you get from a great book or movie. Scripted gameplay in many ways, is excellent at this. But it always nags at me because that kind of gameplay isn’t ‘real’. I wanted to create those same kind of exciting sequences, but between players - no scripting, quests or cutscenes.

There are games that have done this before, which are usually thought of as sandbox mmos - Ultima online, SWG and EVE come to mind. These games are inspiration for a game that I am developing tentatively called, Pulsarc. Pulsarc is a mmo (although It will be sometime, if ever, before the ‘massive’ in mmo is achieved.) Pulsarc will be focused on player-driven adventure and excitement. Here are some of the dreamed features for pulsarc

  • All structures player built and owned - Not only player housing, but player can build cities, shops, fortresses, jails, and more. This will be achieved through a wide variety of placable objects, each having a function of some kind.

  • Open pvp limited - This is a delicate subject. I think open pvp is a big part of the game - because danger creates excitement - just look at movies - the characters are often attacked or come into dangerous situations, which creates excitement. On the flip side of the coin, it is important that the system is fair, and prevents mismatched killing, trolling, and other negative behaviour. This will be one of the biggest design challenges of the game. In the same category of pvp, features like thieving and jails are being considered.

  • More than just combat - While combat will be part of the game, we want the game to be about more than just combat, with main other features being crafting, building, gathering, exploring, city building and maintening.

Those are the core ideas. Future blog will cover various elements of the game progress, including more design, as well as programming and implementation. Heres a screen shot of the game in its current state.
